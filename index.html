Best Zombie Movies of All Time

  • Dawn of the Dead 1978
  • 28 Days Later
  • Shaun of the Dead

Dawn of the Dead 1978 - George Romero

This movie from George Romero just gets it all right. Awesome gore, storyline and characters, and a real sense of dread throughout. The mall they hole up in becomes a character in and of itself and puts a mirror up to late 70s american culture and consumerism. Absolute classic.



28 Days Later

Now these may not be your usual zombified corpses, but honestly they make most zombies look about as dangerous as a kitten in comparison. These guys do not hold back and they will beat the shit out of you as they infect you. Not a fun time.

Cillian Murphy is great in this film as are the ragtag crew he becomes a part of, almost like a new family in the post-apocalypse. Of course when you have infected freaks chasing you and other survivors not being much better, you're going to have a bad time.



Shaun of the Dead

This movie is goddamn hilarious, yet somehow manages to include moments of abject terror and hopelessness, as well as heroism and true friendship. Simon Pegg and Nick Frost have some of the best comedic chemistry ever put on screen, and Edgar Wright knows how to craft a movie with the utmost attention to detail and great editing throughout.

It really just never lets up in any aspect and always feels like a ride the whole way through, you absolutely won't forget this one. Don't forget to check out the other movies in the spiritual trilogy this movie is the start of, "The Cornetto Trilogy".
